## Persona 1: Jake Miller — The Online Gambler

| **Age** | 38 |
| **Occupation** | Warehouse Worker |
| **Device** | Phone |
| **Play Style** | Long sessions, multiple times a day, anywhere |
| **Experience** | Regular casino-goer who switched to online gambling |

### Background
Jake used to visit casinos regularly on weekends, but found it increasingly inconvenient — the drive, the crowds, the dress code. He discovered online slot apps and never looked back. Now he plays on his phone during lunch breaks, after work, and late at night from his couch.

### What He Values
- Casino-like experience from the comfort of home
- High-stakes feeling and big win potential
- Fast spins — wants to play as many rounds as possible
- Jackpots and bonus rounds that replicate the casino rush

### What Frustrates Him
- Low bet limits that don't feel like "real" gambling
- Slow animations wasting his time
- Being reminded of how long he's been playing
- Games that feel too "casual" or cartoon-like

### How He Interprets Feedback
- Treats near-misses as motivation to keep spinning
- Gets more excited and plays longer after a big win
- Ignores session timers and responsible gambling warnings

## Persona 2: Sarah Kim — The Casual Entertainer

| **Age** | 27 |
| **Occupation** | Graphic Designer |
| **Device** | Laptop |
| **Play Style** | Short sessions (10–15 min) during lunch or after work |
| **Experience** | Casual gamer, never played real-money gambling |

### Background
Sarah discovered slot games through a friend and plays occasionally to kill time. She has no interest in real gambling — she just enjoys the colorful visuals, satisfying sounds, and the excitement of not knowing what the next spin will bring.

### What She Values
- Fun visuals and satisfying sound effects
- Simple rules she can understand immediately
- No pressure to spend money
- Light progression (something to unlock or collect)

### What Frustrates Her
- Complicated paytables and confusing symbols
- Feeling like the game is designed to make her lose
- Aggressive ads or popups interrupting gameplay
- Losing progress if she accidentally closes the tab

### How She Interprets Feedback
- Enjoys win celebrations but gets annoyed if losses feel like wins
- Appreciates transparency — wants to know the odds clearly
- Stops playing if the game feels unfair or manipulative
