## Persona 1:  Jake Smith - casual gambler

| **Age** | 21 |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Student |
| **Device** | Iphone, computer(occasionally) |
| **Play Style** | Sessions are very sporatic, ~1-2 sessions per week, 30-60 minute sessions  |
| **Experience** | Casual player, has some experience with other gambling forms but new to online slots |

### Background
Jake has had some experience with gambling since highschool starting with moderate success with sports betting. But now that he is 21, he is able to use more gambling apps legally. His friend put him onto casino apps hanging out. Now whenever he is bored, he will start spinning slots. He puts in whatever spare money he has ranging from $10-100. 
### What He Values
- Quick load-up and play times
- "Big Win" screenshot to show or see what his friends are playing.
- Modern graphics, "hype" music, and satisfying haptic feedback.
- Frequent "small" hits and bonus rounds

### What Frustrates Them
- Large buy ins that drain his balance quickly
- Sleek design, more modern looks rather than classical slots aesthetic
- Constant pop-ups asking for more deposits
- Complex paylines

### How They Interprets Feedback
- Cares less about montary amount,cares more about the aesthetic of the win based on flashiness of the animation.
- Due to his sports betting background, a close spin feel like he is "due" for a win on the next spin.
- He views his $20 balance as a countdown clock for entertainment. If the balance drops slowly with frequent small wins, he interprets the app as "fair" and "giving him a good run."
- Subtle vibrations on his iPhone reinforce the feeling of a "hit," making the experience feel more tangible.


## Persona 2:  Carrie White

| **Age** | 70 |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | retired  |
| **Device** | iphone |
| **Play Style** | 5 sessions per week, 45-60 minute sessions |
| **Experience** | experienced gambler, was a semi-regular casino goer before her health worsened |

### Background
Carrie has been a prolific gambler ever since she retired and she was bored so her and her husband used to go to casinos together to play slots using their spare social security money. Their children are worried about their gambling habits so they try and monitor their spending habits. Ever since their health got worse, they are not able to go to casinos independently and moved in with their child, so their being monitored even harder.

### What They Values
- She values "Classic" slot aesthetics—7s, cherries, and bells. 
- She values daily login bonuses and loyalty rewards that acknowledge her as a "regular."
- High-contrast visuals and large text.
- Features that allow her to play discreetly or manage small amounts of money without triggering "red flags" 

### What Frustrates Them
- "Skill-based" bonus rounds or complicated RPG elements are confusing and feel like "work." 
- Tiny "Close" buttons on pop-up ads or microscopic font sizes for "Terms and Conditions" 
- Hamburger menus or "swipe-up" gestures that aren't clearly marked. 
- Aggressive and high-pitched electronic music 

### How They Interprets Feedback
- She associates specific bell tones and mechanical "clunks" with winning. 
- She interprets 3D-rendered buttons that look like they can be "pushed" much better than flat design.
- She interprets frequent, small-to-medium payouts as the machine being "loose" and "kind," allowing her to stretch her 60-minute session.
- She often interprets excessive pop-ups as a "scam" or a trap, requiring the app to build trust through transparent and simple reward systems.
